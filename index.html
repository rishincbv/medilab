Cardiology

Cardiologists use various diagnostic tools and tests to evaluate heart conditions, including electrocardiograms (ECG), echocardiograms, stress tests, cardiac catheterization, and coronary angiography. They may also prescribe medications, perform procedures like angioplasty or stent placement, and provide lifestyle recommendations to manage heart disease.

Neurology

To diagnose and treat these and other neurological disorders, neurologists may perform a range of procedures and tests, including neurological examinations, imaging studies (such as MRI or CT scans), electroencephalograms (EEGs), nerve conduction studies, and lumbar punctures

Hepatology

Treatment in hepatology may involve lifestyle modifications, medication management, antiviral therapies for viral hepatitis, immunosuppressive agents for autoimmune liver diseases, interventions for liver complications, liver transplantation, and ongoing monitoring of liver function.

Pediatrics

Pediatricians work closely with parents and caregivers to provide comprehensive care for children. They communicate with families, educate them about their child's health, and address any concerns or questions they may have. They also collaborate with other healthcare professionals, such as pediatric subspecialists, to manage complex medical conditions or refer patients for specialized care when needed.
